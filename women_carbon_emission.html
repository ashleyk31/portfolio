[Women in Government and Carbon Emissions] Exploring a Country's Relationship Between Variables

[ TIMELINE ]

August 2023 - December 2023

[ TOOLS ]

R

ggplot

ANOVA

[ STORY ]

Since the 1970's, global carbon emissions have been increasing, contributing to global warming. The amount of carbon each country emits is regulated by their parliament. Yet, there is hardly any research on how gender identity affects the attitudes surrounding climate change which may pertain to how a country decides to handle their carbon emissions. Most studies focus on other variables, but, we know men and women experience stem education and other social factors differently; meaning concerns that reflect their climate change attitude may differ. So my three teammates and I wanted to explore the possible relationship between women's representation in office and the way a country handles climate issues. We were able to conduct statistical tests to identify the correlation between the amount of CO2 a country emits and the percentage of women in their parliament.
