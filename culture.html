Culture

Year 2021

This series of miniature photography captures the unique Korean culture through origami.

PUBLISHED

http://hcpssjuniorseniorexhibition2021.weebly.com/

"HCPSS 31st Annual Senior Show & Junior Portfolio Development Exhibition"

ARTIST STATEMENT

My sustained investigation focuses on the unique Korean culture through their unique colors, unique texture, and ancient tradition designs. After researching through multiple media, I produced a list of the most used unique color combinations, items, and designs that appeared throughout them to create a miniature figure using paper and other items to express the unique texture as much as possible. My focus was on the colors, which were also known as the Obangsaek, which means five colors including blue, red, yellow, white, and black. Hoping to capture the details of each item, I used different lightings, textures of paper, backgrounds, and properties to allow the viewers to fully observe the object and the culture. There are a total of ten photographs: the Korean dish (kimbap), traditional Korean letters (hangul), traditional lanterns along the unique roof design (Cheongsa chorong, Giwa), traditional fans, traditional folding screen (byeong-poong), another famous Korean dish (bibimbap), traditional board game (Yun nori), another traditional roof design (Giwa), traditional clothing (hanbok), and a modern drinking environment with the silver table and the kimchi side dish. I hope people can view this series not only to see the details in each photograph but also to recognize the culture and to understand that these are all part of the unique Korean culture that should be acknowledged.
